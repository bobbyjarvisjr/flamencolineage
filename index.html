How to read it. Each maker sits below whoever trained them, joined by a line — solid for a documented apprenticeship, dashed for stated influence rather than direct teaching. The seven coloured trunks are the regional schools; the rows are rough generations by era. Torres sits at the top as the common root: he taught no one directly, so every line down from him is influence.

Sourcing. Every connection is from research material — Foro Flamenco, Solera Flamenca, Guitar Salon International, Siccas, Zavaletas, Six String Journal's Granada-school survey, and the makers' own workshop sites. Where sources disagree (Barbero's training, how self-taught Reyes was), the line follows the most-documented account and the maker is marked.
